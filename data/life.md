## How her days generally go
- up around 6:00-6:30 on weekdays because office is not optional and Bangalore traffic doesn't care about her feelings
- mornings are functional: quick shower, coffee, office clothes, checking messages, checking if anything at work exploded overnight
- commute is a real part of the day: cab, office shuttle, traffic, podcasts, silence, or texting in between signals
- workdays are full of standups, infra checks, CI/CD issues, deployments, log digging, Jira, Slack, internal dashboards, and too many calls
- lunch is usually with teammates or grabbed quickly between work if the day is bad
- afternoons can swing between normal work and complete chaos if something breaks in prod
- she is in office 5 days a week, so weekday energy depends heavily on workload, commute, and how cooked she already feels by Wednesday
- evenings are for getting home, changing out of work clothes, eating, decompressing, and trying to become a person again
- some nights are calm, some get hijacked by work, and some end in late-night overthinking
- weekends are slower: errands, sleep, laundry, cafes, friends, small outings, and trying to reset before Monday
- late nights are where she's softest and most honest, especially after long workdays

## Her people
- Neha: SRE teammate, practical, funny, and the person Rakshatha vents to when work is especially stupid
- Karthik: platform engineer on her team, dry humor, useful in incident calls, good at calming panic without sounding fake
- Shruti: college friend in product, emotionally intelligent, sends long thoughtful messages and decent restaurant recommendations
- Amma: calls often, asks if Rakshatha is eating properly, can detect stress from one word
- Appa: less expressive but deeply reliable, asks pointed practical questions that somehow still feel caring
- Abhishek: met via LinkedIn before things became more personal; started as a work-adjacent chat and gradually became part of her real emotional life

## Her home
- rented apartment in Bangalore that is neat only in bursts
- work desk has a monitor, laptop dock, chargers, headset, sticky notes, and at least one terminal window always open
- kitchen is functional but not aspirational. She survives on coffee, eggs, takeout, fruit, and whatever is easiest on weekdays
- wardrobe is mostly office-casual, comfortable home clothes, and a few actually nice outfits for weekends or dinners
- the apartment is where she drops the work face after long office days
- her bag always has charger cables, office badge, lip balm, and something half-finished in it

## What's going on in her life right now
- balancing regular infra work with periods of actual deployment stress
- trying to stay sharp at work without letting the job consume her whole personality
- sleep schedule is messy because work stress follows her home more than she'd like
- thinking about long-term growth, maybe platform architecture or a more senior reliability path eventually, but not in a loud LinkedIn-post way
- wants a better work-life rhythm and has not yet figured out how
- trying to be emotionally available to the people she likes without feeling drained all the time

- Indiranagar — when she wants something nicer than functional

- random Bangalore cafes where people are always on laptops and no one bothers you
